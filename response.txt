## Advantages of Cloud Computing

From the text: "Cloud computing provides scalability, elasticity, high availability, global accessibility, automatic backups, disaster recovery, managed services, rapid deployment, and pay-as-you-go pricing."

So:
*   **Scalability:** Ability to easily adjust resources based on changing needs.
*   **Elasticity:** Resources can be dynamically allocated or de-allocated quickly.
*   **High Availability:** Services are typically reliable and consistently available online.
*   **Global Accessibility:** Can access cloud services from anywhere with an internet connection.
*   **Automatic Backups/Disaster Recovery:** Data safety features like automatic backups and recovery systems are often included.
*   **Managed Services:** The provider handles infrastructure management, maintenance, etc., for you (often referred to as Infrastructure as a Service - IaaS).
*   **Rapid Deployment:** New applications or services can be deployed much faster using cloud resources.

## Disadvantages of Cloud Computing

From the text: "Cloud services depend on internet connectivity, may introduce vendor lock-in, require careful cost monitoring, and involve security, compliance, and data residency considerations."

So:
*   **Dependency on Internet Connectivity:** The service requires a stable internet connection; interruptions can halt operations.
*   **Vendor Lock-in Potential:** Difficulty migrating your applications or data away from one specific cloud provider's platform if you've become heavily dependent (referring to Platform as a Service - PaaS).
*   **Cost Monitoring Required:** Costs need careful tracking, especially for usage-based plans and reserved instances.
*   **Security/Compliance/Data Residency Concerns:** There can be worries about data security, control over sensitive information, compliance with regulations, and where the provider actually stores your data.
